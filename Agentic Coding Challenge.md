# **Agentic Coding Challenge (60 Minutes)**

You will be given:

* 1 hour  
* Access only to Kimi K2.6

Your task is to build as much of the Harbor.Space website and application stack as possible. Possible areas include:

* Homepage and marketing pages  
* Program pages  
* Calls-to-action and lead capture flows  
* Authentication  
* Logged-out and logged-in experiences  
* Mock CRM integration  
* Analytics/tracking  
* Mock email workflows  
* Any other supporting functionality you consider important

At the end of the hour, submit:

1. Your repository  
2. Your OpenCode JSON logs (inside your repository)

Evaluation criteria:

* Agentic coding effectiveness  
  * How well you steer the agent  
  * Task decomposition  
  * Parallelization of work  
  * Decision-making under time pressure  
* Code quality  
  * Taste and judgment  
  * Simplicity  
  * Maintainability  
  * Absence of AI-generated slop  
* Prioritization  
  * Choosing the highest-value work  
  * Shipping polished functionality rather than unfinished breadth

There is intentionally too little time to complete everything.

Quality over quantity. One feature implemented exceptionally well is preferable to many features implemented poorly. We are interested in how you prioritize, make trade-offs, and collaborate with an AI coding agent under pressure.

Along with your repository, submit the relevant OpenCode session JSON files (typically found under `~/.local/share/opencode/storage/`) so your agent interactions, task decomposition, and workflow can be reviewed.
